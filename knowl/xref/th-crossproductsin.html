Theorem 1.5.12.

Let \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\) be vectors in \(\R^3\text{.}\) Let \(\theta\) be the angle between \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\) such that \(0\leq\theta\leq \pi\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \norm{\mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}}=\norm{\mathbf{u}}\norm{\mathbf{v}}\sin\theta. \end{equation*}

Proof.

By LemmaÂ 1.5.11 and (1.5.1) we have
\begin{align*} \norm{\mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}}^2\amp =\norm{\mathbf{u}}^2\norm{\mathbf{v}}^2-(\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v})^2 \\ \amp =\norm{\mathbf{u}}^2\norm{\mathbf{v}}^2-(\norm{\mathbf{u}}\norm{\mathbf{v}}\cos \theta)^2 \\ \amp =\norm{\mathbf{u}}^2\norm{\mathbf{v}}^2-\norm{\mathbf{u}}^2\norm{\mathbf{v}}^2\cos^2\theta \\ \amp =\norm{\mathbf{u}}^2\norm{\mathbf{v}}^2(1-\cos^2\theta) \\ \amp =\norm{\mathbf{u}}^2\norm{\mathbf{v}}^2\sin^2\theta \end{align*}
Observe that all magnitudes are non-negative. Also, \(\sin\theta\geq 0\) because \(0\leq\theta\leq \pi\text{.}\) Taking the square root of both sides give us
\begin{equation*} \norm{\mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}}=\norm{\mathbf{u}}\norm{\mathbf{v}}\sin\theta \end{equation*}
