Exercise 5.
The lines, \(x+3y=1\) and \(4x-y=3.\) intersect at \(x=\frac{10}{13},y=\frac{1}{13}\text{.}\) Use Jacobi’s method and the Gauss-Seidel method with an initial guess of \(x=0,y=0\) to find an approximate solution where each coordinate is within 0.1 of the exact solution. Write the coefficient matrix so it is strictly diagonally dominant in order to ensure convergence.
