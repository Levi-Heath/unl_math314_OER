Theorem 6.3.11.

Let \(T:\R^n\rightarrow \R^n\) be a linear transformation, and let \(M\) be the standard matrix of \(T\text{.}\)
  1. (Existence of Inverses.) \(T\) is invertible if and only if \(M\) is invertible. If \(T\) is invertible, then the inverse is induced by \(M^{-1}\text{.}\)
  2. (Uniqueness of Inverses.) If \(S\) is an inverse of \(T\text{,}\) then \(S\) is unique.

Proof.

Part Item 1 follows directly from the preceding discussion. Part Item 2 follows from uniqueness of matrix inverses. ( Theorem 4.4.2.)
