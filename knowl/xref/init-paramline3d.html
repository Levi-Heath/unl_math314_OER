Exploration 1.3.4.

Consider the following set of parametric equations
\begin{align*} x\amp=4t+1 \\ y\amp=8t-3 \\ z\amp=5t+1 \end{align*}
We will begin by making a table of values.
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|} \hline t\amp x \amp y \amp z\\ \hline -1 \amp -3 \amp -11 \amp -4\\ \hline 0 \amp 1 \amp -3 \amp 1 \\ \hline 1 \amp 5\amp 5 \amp 6\\ \hline 2 \amp 9\amp 13 \amp 11\\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}
Observe that every time \(t\) increases by \(1\text{,}\) \(x\) increases by \(4\text{,}\) \(y\) increases by \(8\text{,}\) and \(z\) increases by \(5\text{.}\) The pattern ``out \(4\text{,}\) over \(8\text{,}\) up \(5\)" is illustrated in the diagram.
We will use a direction vector \(\mathbf{v}=\begin{bmatrix}4\\8\\5\end{bmatrix}\) to represent the direction of the line. Note that the components of \(\mathbf{v}\) are the same as the coefficients of \(t\) in the parametric equations.
\begin{align*} x\amp={\color{red}4}t+1 \\ y\amp={\color{red}8}t-3 \\ z\amp={\color{red}5}t+1 \end{align*}
Also, observe that the constants \(1, -3, 1\) correspond to the coordinates of the point on the line for which \(t=0\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} x\amp=4t+{\color{red}1} \\ y\amp=8t+({\color{red}-3}) \\ z\amp=5t+{\color{red}1} \end{align*}
As before, the point \((1, -3, 1)\) may be replaced with any other point on the line to produce a different set of parametric equations describing the same line.
